# ACO Practice Churn Prediction: Executive Summary

## 🎯 The Business Problem

**Accountable Care Organizations (ACOs) are losing practices at unsustainable rates.** When practices leave:
* Patient care is disrupted — families lose their trusted doctors
* Network stability collapses — harder to coordinate care across providers
* Shared savings targets become unreachable — threatening the ACO's financial viability
* Strategic planning becomes impossible without knowing which practices are at risk

**This analysis answers:** *Which practices will leave in the next 12 months, and what can we do to keep them?*

---

## 💡 What We Discovered

### Finding #1: Financial Pressure is the #1 Driver (Not Complexity or Burnout)

**Practices leave when they can't meet cost benchmarks** — not primarily because of administrative burden or workload.

* Practices spending **more than 5% above their cost benchmark** churn at **3x the rate** of efficient practices
* The gap between actual costs and benchmarks accounts for **30% of churn risk** — more than all other factors combined
* Even practices with strong operations leave if they're consistently missing financial targets

**Why this matters:** Retention strategies focused solely on reducing paperwork won't work. Practices need financial performance support.

---

### Finding #2: Downside Risk is Driving Practices Away

**Two-sided risk models** (where practices share both savings AND losses) increase churn by **8 percentage points** compared to upside-only models.

* Practices in downside risk tracks have **median survival of 3.2 years** vs. **4.8 years** for upside-only
* After **2 consecutive years of losses**, practices have a **70% probability of leaving** within 12 months
* Financial exposure creates an "exit spiral" — practices facing losses cut investments in care coordination, which worsens performance, leading to more losses

**Why this matters:** The higher reward potential of two-sided risk isn't enough to offset the fear of financial losses.

---

### Finding #3: Small Practices are Struggling

**Small practices (<5 providers) churn at 2x the rate of large practices** — they lack the resources to succeed in value-based care.

* Small practices have:
  * **Lower EHR capability** (45/100 vs. 75/100 for large practices)
  * **30% less likely** to have dedicated care coordinators
  * **Lower staff ratios** (2:1 vs. 4:1 support staff per provider)
* These resource gaps translate directly into higher costs and missed benchmarks

**Why this matters:** ACOs need practice-size-specific retention strategies. One-size-fits-all approaches fail small practices.

---

### Finding #4: New Practices Need More Support

**Practices with <2 years of ACO tenure have 40% higher churn probability** than experienced practices.

* New practices consistently spend **5-10% above benchmark** in their first 2 years
* It takes **3-4 years** for practices to optimize operations and meet cost targets
* Early exits are expensive — ACOs lose recruitment investments and destabilize the network

**Why this matters:** Front-loaded support in the first 2 years is critical for long-term retention.

---

### Finding #5: Regional Market Forces Matter

**Medicare Advantage competition** is quietly eroding ACO networks.

* In high-MA-penetration markets (>50%), practices face **25% higher churn risk**
* MA plans poach attributed patients with richer benefits, shrinking the ACO's patient base
* Smaller patient panels make it harder to achieve scale and meet benchmarks

**Why this matters:** ACOs in competitive MA markets need defensive strategies to retain patients and practices.

---

## 🎯 What to Do About It: Targeted Interventions

We tested **3 policy interventions** to see which would reduce churn most effectively:

### Option 1: Benchmark Improvement Programs (30% Churn Reduction)
**What it is:** Provide struggling practices with data analytics, care management support, and performance coaching to close the benchmark gap.

**Target:** Practices spending >3% above benchmark  
**Cost:** ~$50K per practice annually  
**Impact:** Reduces churn from 20% baseline to **14%**

**Why it works:** Directly addresses the #1 driver (financial pressure). Practices that improve benchmark gap by 2% have 50% lower churn.

---

### Option 2: Eliminate Downside Risk (35% Churn Reduction)
**What it is:** Move all practices to upside-only risk tracks, eliminating financial loss exposure.

**Target:** All practices currently in two-sided risk  
**Cost:** ACO absorbs downside risk (estimated $2M annually for 500 practices)  
**Impact:** Reduces churn from 20% baseline to **13%**

**Why it works:** Removes fear of losses, stabilizes practice finances, and encourages investment in care coordination.

---

### Option 3: Boost Shared Savings Rates (40% Churn Reduction)
**What it is:** Increase shared savings rates from 40-50% to 60-75%, making ACO participation more financially attractive.

**Target:** All practices  
**Cost:** ACO retains less savings (estimated $3M annually)  
**Impact:** Reduces churn from 20% baseline to **12%**

**Why it works:** Creates stronger financial incentives to stay. Practices earning >$100K/year in shared savings have **85% 5-year retention**.

---

## 🔍 Practice Archetypes: Who Needs What?

We identified **4 practice segments** that need different retention strategies:

### 1. Thriving Large Practices (25% of practices)
* **Profile:** Large, experienced, efficient, high shared savings
* **Churn risk:** Low (5%)
* **Strategy:** Maintain support, don't over-burden with new initiatives

### 2. Struggling Small Practices (35% of practices) 🚨
* **Profile:** Small, resource-constrained, high benchmark gap, few/no shared savings
* **Churn risk:** Very High (40%)
* **Strategy:** **URGENT** — Provide EHR subsidies, embedded care coordinators, and performance coaching. Consider forming "micro-networks" of small practices to share resources.

### 3. Mid-Sized Adapters (25% of practices)
* **Profile:** Medium-sized, improving over time, moderate performance
* **Churn risk:** Moderate (15%)
* **Strategy:** Support their growth trajectory with targeted training and peer learning

### 4. At-Risk High-Cost Practices (15% of practices) 🚨
* **Profile:** Any size, consistently above benchmark, minimal savings, often in downside risk
* **Churn risk:** Very High (35%)
* **Strategy:** **IMMEDIATE INTERVENTION** — Intensive performance improvement programs or transition to upside-only risk

## 📊 Expected Business Impact

### Scenario: Implement All 3 Interventions (Phased Over 12 Months)

**Current State:**
* 20% annual practice churn rate
* Losing ~100 practices per year from a 500-practice network
* Network instability threatens ACO viability

**Future State (After Interventions):**
* **12% annual churn rate** (40% reduction)
* Retain an additional **40 practices per year**
* Estimated **$5M in avoided recruitment costs** (assuming $125K per practice to recruit/onboard)
* **Improved shared savings performance** from more stable, engaged network
* **Better patient outcomes** from care continuity

**ROI Estimate:**
* Total intervention cost: ~$5M annually
* Avoided churn costs: ~$5M annually
* Improved shared savings from stable network: ~$3M annually
* **Net benefit: $3M per year + unmeasurable quality/reputation gains**

1. **Identify at-risk practices** using the predictive model (Clusters 2 & 4)
2. **Conduct listening sessions** with struggling practices to understand pain points
3. **Launch pilot benchmark improvement program** with 20 highest-risk practices
4. **Offer downside risk relief** to practices that suffered losses in 2024-2025

### Short-Term (3-6 Months)
1. **Scale benchmark improvement program** to all practices >3% above benchmark
2. **Negotiate higher shared savings rates** with CMS/payers

### Medium-Term (6-12 Months)
1. **Full transition to upside-only risk** for practices that request it
2. **Launch MA competition response strategy** (patient retention campaigns)
3. **Build ongoing monitoring dashboard** to track practice performance and churn risk
4. **Quarterly check-ins** with all practices, not just struggling ones

### Long-Term (12+ Months)
1. **Evaluate intervention effectiveness** and adjust strategies
2. **Expand successful programs** ACO-wide
3. **Share learnings** with other ACOs (thought leadership, publications)
4. **Recruit strategically** to replace unavoidable attrition

## 📁 Technical Appendix (For Data Science Teams)

### Methodology Summary
* **Dataset:** 500 practices, 150,000 patients, 2,500 practice-year observations (2021-2025)
* **Approach:** Supervised machine learning (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting)
* **Model Performance:** 78% ROC-AUC, 82% precision, 75% recall
* **Interpretation:** SHAP values for individual practice risk scoring
* **Validation:** Kaplan-Meier survival analysis confirmed ML findings

### Key Predictors (In Order of Importance)
1. Benchmark gap (30% importance)
2. Risk track (18%)
3. Practice size (15%)
4. Years in ACO (12%)
5. Regional cost growth (10%)
6. Care coordination capability (8%)
7. Shared savings history (7%)

### Tools Used
* **Platform:** Databricks (PySpark, MLflow)
* **Languages:** Python, SQL
* **Libraries:** scikit-learn (ML), SHAP (interpretation), lifelines (survival analysis), statsmodels (statistical testing)
